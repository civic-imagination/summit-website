speakers

Indy Johar

Co-founder of 00 & Dark Matter Labs

Indy is a London-based architect and designer who helps multinational companies and institutions transition to a positive systems economy. He's contributed to research projects including the Compendium for the Civic Economy, and supports experiments around open-source architecture, the future of cities, and new forms of governance.

Jen Sukis

Adjunct Professor, University of Texas

Jen works at the intersection of user-centered design and artificial intelligence, leading the creative team for IBM Watson and teaching Advanced Design for AI at the UT’s Center for Integrated Design, enabling strategists, designers, and engineers to use cognitive tools to create transformative and compelling AI experiences.

Paul Stey

Director of Data Science, Brown University

Paul leads the data science practices at Brown University, supporting data-intensive computing, machine learning, informatics, reporting, and analytics in collaboration with governments at the state and local level. He was previously a Mathematical Statistician for the US Environmental Protection Agency.

Mari Nakano

Design Director, NYC Service Design Studio

Mari leads the Service Design Studio for the New York City Mayor's Office of Economic Opportunity, which uses evidence-based research and innovation to and decrease poverty and increase equity in New York City. She was previously design & interaction lead for the Office of Innovation at UNICEF.

David Yokum

Director, The Policy Lab

David leads The Policy Lab, which is creating a new model for how university researchers can collaborate with all levels of government. He was previously a founding member of the White House Social & Behavioral Sciences Team and director of The Lab @ DC, one of the first scientific labs within a local government.

Jake Dunagan

Director, Governance Futures Lab

Jake is an experiential futurist, governance designer, and teacher. He leads the Governance Futures Lab at the Institute for the Future, which brings together futures thinkers, technologists, philanthropists, designers, policymakers, and social innovators to re-imagine governance for the 21st century.
